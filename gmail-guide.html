How to Use Your Nordible Business Email Directly Inside Gmail

While webmail clients handle the basics, many professionals prefer the interface, search capabilities, and familiarity of Gmail. With Nordible Mail, you don't have to sacrifice your professional domain name to use Google's platform.

By linking your Nordible instance with a personal Gmail account, you can read, organize, and reply to client messages using your business identity (you@yourbusiness.com) seamlessly from the Gmail interface.

The Strategy: Seamless Two-Way Integration

To get a fast, real-time email experience, we avoid Gmail's old POP3 fetching method (which can introduce 15–30 minute delays). Instead, we will configure a two-part system:

  • Incoming Mail: Nordible instantly routes incoming business emails to Gmail via a secure server-level forwarder.
  • Outgoing Mail: Gmail routes your replies back through Nordible’s SMTP server, ensuring your professional domain passes all modern security checks (SPF, DKIM) and never shows up as "via gmail.com".

Part 1: Instant Routing for Incoming Mail

First, we need to make sure that any email hitting your Nordible server immediately forwards a copy to your Gmail inbox.

  1. Log into your Nordible Mail UI using your mailbox credentials.
  2. Navigate to Email > Configuration > Mailboxes.
  3. Find your specific business mailbox in the list and click the Edit button next to it.
  4. Scroll down to the Forwarding section.
  5. In the fields provided, add your personal @gmail.com address.

Crucial Step

Ensure that "Keep a copy in the mailbox" remains checked. This keeps a safe, permanent archive on your own Nordible server as a backup.

Test it: Send an email from a third account to your business address. It should instantly land in your Gmail inbox.

Part 2: Configuring Gmail to "Send Mail As" Your Domain

With incoming mail working, the next step is authorizing Gmail to send messages on behalf of your business domain using Nordible’s infrastructure.

Step 1

Access Gmail Settings

  • Open Gmail on a desktop web browser.
  • Click the Gear icon in the upper right corner and select See all settings.
  • Select the Accounts and Import tab.
  • Locate the "Send mail as:" section and click Add another email address. A small yellow configuration window will pop up.
Step 2

Set Your Identity

  • Name: Enter the name you want recipients to see (e.g., Kabeer Shah).
  • Email address: Type your complete business email address (you@yourbusiness.com).
  • Leave "Treat as an alias" checked.
  • Click Next Step.
Step 3

Authenticate with Nordible SMTP

To prevent your emails from hitting your clients' spam folders, Gmail must log directly into your server. Enter your details exactly as follows:

SMTP Server: mail.nordible.co
Port: 465
Username: you@yourbusiness.com
Password: Your email account password
Secured connection: SSL (Required for Port 465)

Click Add Account.

Step 4

Complete the Verification

Google will now issue a verification request to ensure you own the domain.

  • Because you successfully completed Part 1, the Google verification email will appear right in your Gmail inbox.
  • Open the email, copy the numerical verification code, paste it into the yellow popup window, and click Verify.

Tailoring Gmail for a Professional Workflow

To ensure you don't accidentally reply to a client from your personal email address, make one final adjustment:

  1. Return to Settings > Accounts and Import.
  2. Under the "Send mail as" section, look for "When replying to a message:".
  3. Change this option to "Reply from the same address the message was sent to".

With this toggle active, Gmail intelligently adapts. If a client writes to your business domain, hitting reply automatically switches your outbound address—saving you manual steps and keeping your professional branding perfectly intact.
