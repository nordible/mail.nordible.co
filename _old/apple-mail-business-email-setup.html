Integrating Your Nordible Business Email with Apple Mail (iOS & macOS)

Apple Mail handles Nordible Mail wonderfully, but there is a major architectural difference between Apple's mobile devices (iPhone/iPad) and their desktop computers (Macs):

  • iOS/iPadOS natively supports Exchange ActiveSync, meaning it can pull your email, calendar, and contacts instantly with push notifications.
  • macOS does not support ActiveSync for third-party servers. On a Mac, you sync your mail via standard IMAP, while your calendars and contacts sync via CalDAV/CardDAV.

To bridge this gap seamlessly, Nordible Mail offers an automated feature: a downloadable Configuration Profile (.mobileconfig) file. This bundle automatically tells your device exactly how to connect without you needing to memorize ports or hostnames.

Method 1: The One-Click Configuration Profile (Highly Recommended)

Using the Nordible configuration script ensures your mail, calendar, and global address book are all set up perfectly in under a minute.

On iPhone or iPad (iOS)
  1. Open Safari on your phone and log into your Nordible Mail UI.
  2. Tap the menu and look for your profile/client configuration guides.
  3. Tap the option to download the customized configuration profile.
    Pro Tip: Choose the option that appends an App Password directly into the profile for zero-friction login.
  4. A system alert will say: "The website is trying to download a configuration profile..." Tap Allow.
  5. Open your iPhone's main Settings app.
  6. Tap Profile Downloaded near the top.
  7. Tap Install, enter your device passcode, and confirm.
  8. Enter your email password when prompted and tap Next.
On a Mac (macOS)
  1. Open Safari and log into your Nordible Mail UI.
  2. Download the .mobileconfig file from the setup guides section.
  3. Double-click the file in your Downloads folder.
  4. Open System Settings and search for Profiles.
  5. Double-click the Nordible profile, click Install..., and confirm with your Mac’s admin password.
  6. Enter your mailbox password when requested.

Method 2: Manual IMAP/SMTP Setup

If you prefer not to install profiles, you can configure the native applications manually by feeding them the direct connection coordinates.

Manual Steps
  1. Open Settings (or System Settings on Mac).
  2. Go to Mail > Accounts > Add Account.
  3. Choose Other, then select Add Mail Account.
  4. Fill out your identity details:
    • Name: Your display name
    • Email: you@yourbusiness.com
    • Password: Your mailbox password
  5. Tap Next and ensure IMAP is selected.
  6. Populate the server details as follows:
Section Parameter Required Value
Incoming (IMAP) Host Name mail.nordible.co
User Name Your full email address
Password Your mailbox password
Outgoing (SMTP) Host Name mail.nordible.co
User Name Your full email address
Password Your mailbox password

Tap Next or Save to verify.

Crucial Apple Mail Warning

Apple Mail includes a feature called "Automatically manage connection settings". If your emails suddenly stop sending or receiving, check this setting. Apple sometimes resets the SMTP port from 587/465 to 25, which breaks secure authentication. If you run into issues, turn that switch off and verify your outgoing port is correct.
